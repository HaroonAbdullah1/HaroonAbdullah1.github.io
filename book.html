To Kill a Mockingbird

Summary

"To Kill a Mockingbird" is a novel set in the 1930s in the fictional town of Maycomb, Alabama. It follows the story of Scout Finch, a young girl, and her brother Jem, as they navigate the complex issues of race and class in their community. Their father, Atticus Finch, is a lawyer who defends a black man accused of rape, despite facing opposition from his fellow townspeople.

About the Author

Harper Lee was an American novelist born in 1926 in Monroeville, Alabama. "To Kill a Mockingbird" was her only published book, and it won the Pulitzer Prize in 1961. Lee passed away in 2016 at the age of 89.

Publication Date

"To Kill a Mockingbird" was first published in 1960.
